Novak Djokovic’s mother, Dijana Đoković, raises concerns regarding the mistreatment her son is facing from the Australian government. Learn more about what exactly is happening.

Novak Djokovic is a Serbian tennis player, ranked as number 1 globally by the Association of Tennis Professionals.

Novak began his professional career in 2003 and, over the years, has achieved a combined record of 20 single Grand Slam titles, including a record nine Australian Open titles.

Currently, Novak is being held in a detention center after his visa application was denied, and his family is not happy.

He was quarantined after being unable to show Vaccination proof and is being mistreated with poor living accommodations like a prisoner, as mentioned by his family.

Who Is Novak Djokovic Mother Dijana Đoković?

Novak Djokovic’s mother, Dijana Đoković, is of Croatian descent who owns a fast-food joint with her husband, Srdjan Djokovic.

Novak is the firstborn child of Srdjan and Dijana, born on 22 May 1987 in Belgrade, SR Serbia, SFR Yugoslavia.

Just days ago, Dijana and her family shared their distress about her son is being treated like a prisoner in poor conditions in his quarantine hotel. He is in a detention center after his visa application was denied, and the immigration hotel where he is kept is dirty and filled with bugs.

Dijana, who has always supported her son, is sad to know that he is living under such conditions and is not given a chance to move to a better hotel.

Moreover, he has received moldy bread, disgusting food and cannot get fresh air. It is monitored 24/7, and windows cannot be opened at any time.

Dijana is considered the Virgin Mary since she was the driving force behind Novak’s success. According to him, she is the mother of the new age, having given birth to one of the most powerful Serbs.

Novak Djokovic Father Srđan Đoković And Siblings In The Family

Novak Djokovic’s father, Srdjan Djokovic, was a professional skier and ski instructor. He first encountered the sport while working in the highlands of Serbia, where he opened a cafe and sold sports equipment.

Novak is also of Serbian descent along with Croatian. After his skiing career, his father started owning a restaurant and sports equipment in the mountains of Serbia with his wife, Diana.

Novak has two younger brothers, Marko and Djordje Djokovic, who also play professional tennis.

Mark was named the fifth player in the Serbian Davis Cup squad for the quarterfinal tie against Croatia in the 2010 Davis Cup.

Djordje’s highest achievement so far reached two quarterfinals at the 2015 China Open.

What Did Novak Djokovic Parents Say To The Australian Government?

Novak Djokovic’s parents, Srdjan and Dijana Djokovic, have lashed out at Australian authorities for tormenting their son and keeping him hostage. After canceling the world No one tennis star’s visa, they have kept him as a prisoner in a poor environment, as per their claims.

The 34-year-old was denied entry to the country after arriving at Melbourne airport on Wednesday night over a visa issue involving a team member.

Victorian officials then questioned Djokovic for hours about the medical release he received from the Covid-19 vaccine, which seemed to give him a chance to fly and enter this month’s Australian Open tournament in Melbourne Park.

They further claim that they’re stomping all over him to stomp all over Serbia and the Serbian people. They have dared attack Novak to bring Serbia to its knees.

They say he is being humiliated by Australian politicians and used as an example to show how the Australian government is focused on border protection.
