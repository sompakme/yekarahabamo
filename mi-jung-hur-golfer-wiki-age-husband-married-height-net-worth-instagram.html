Mi Jung Hur Biography – Mi Jung Hur Wiki

Mi Jung Hur (M. J. Hur) is a South Korean female professional golfer. She currently plays on the LPGA Tour and previously played on the Futures Tour and the Ladies European Tour (LET). Hur began playing golf at the age of 10. As an amateur, she was part of the Korean National Team in 2005 and 2006, winning the individual and team competition at the Korea National Team Festival. In 2006, Hur won the individual title at the Queen Sirikit Cup Asia-Pacific tournament and was a member of the third-place team. Hur earned a sponsor’s exemption to play at the 2006 KOLON – Hana Bank Championship where she finished in a tie for 6th.

Hur turned professional in December 2007 and played on Duramed Futures Tour in 2008. She recorded 8 top-10 finishes, including a win at the Louisiana Pelican Classic in April 2008. She finished 4th on the Duramed FUTURES Tour season-ending money list to earn Priority List Category 9 for the 2009 LPGA season. Hur joined the LPGA Tour in 2009 and during her rookie year, she defeated Norwegian Suzann Pettersen and American Michele Redman in a two-hole, sudden-death playoff to win her first LPGA event at the Safeway Classic.

She won four times on the LPGA Tour including at the Safeway Classic in August 2009, on September 21, 2014, she won at the Yokohama Tire LPGA Classic. She won by 4 strokes over American Stacy Lewis. On August 11, 2019, Hur won at the Aberdeen Standard Investments Ladies Scottish Open after defeating Thai Moriya Jutanugarn and South Korean Lee Jeong-eun. On September 29, 2019, Hur won at the Indy Women in Tech Championship by 4 strokes over Danish Nanna Koerstz Madsen. When she is not on the golf course, Hur enjoys playing computer games.
