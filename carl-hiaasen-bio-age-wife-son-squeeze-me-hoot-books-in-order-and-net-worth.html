Carl Hiaasen Biography

Carl Hiaasen is an American multiple award-winning Author, Journalist and Novelist who was born and brought-up in Plantation, Florida. As a Journalist, he worked for TODAY (Cocoa, Florida) as a reporter after which he joined the ‘Miami Herald’ newspaper, working for the city desk, Sunday magazine and award-winning investigative team.

Carl also worked as a columnist for the newspaper since 1985 to March 2021. Due to his notable and successful work, his columns have been collected in three published volumes named Kick Ass in 1999, Paradise Screwed in 2001 and Dance of the Reptiles in 2014. As an Author, he bases his books on the following subjects; environmentalism, political corruption, fraudsters and Florida. Some of his novels include ‘Squeeze Me’, ‘Hoot’ and ‘Flush’.

Carl Hiaasen Education

Hiaasen attended and graduated from Plantation High School in 1970. He joined Emory University, where he worked as a contributor of the satirical humor columns to the student-run newspaper The Emory Wheel. He then transferred to the University of Florida from which he graduated with a degree in journalism in 1974. While in the University of Florida, he wrote for the University’s ‘The Independent Florida Alligator’.

Carl Hiaasen Family, Parents and Siblings

Carl was born to Patricia and Odel Hiaasen in Plantation, Florida. Carl Hiaasen Hoot

Carl recommends Hoot (a 2002 mystery/suspense novel) for ages 9-12. The setting takes place in Florida, where new arrival Roy makes two oddball friends and a bad enemy and joins an effort to stop construction of a pancake house which would destroy a colony of burrowing owls who live on the site. The themes in the novel are friendship, teamwork, growing up, corruption, parental love, kinship, environmentalism, and integrity.

The character goes through different adventures to get here. Furthermore, a film adaptation of the book was released on May 2006, starring Luke Wilson as Officer Delinko, Logan Lerman as Roy Eberhart, Brie Larson as Beatrice Leep, Tim Blake Nelson as Leroy Brannit, Neil Flynn as Mr. Eberhart, Robert Wagner as Mayor Grandy, Cody Linley as Napoleon Leep, and Clark Gregg as Chuck Muckle while Hiaasen portrays Muckle’s assistant Felix.

Carl Hiaasen Squeeze Me

Squeeze Me is his latest adult book which was published on 25 August 2020. The book debuted at #2 on the New York Times Combined Print and E-Book Print Best Sellers List. The novel is about the glitzy Palm Beach social season, and features wild pythons and a fictional, well-fed U.S. president who has a vacation mansion on the island. Squeeze Me was listed as one of the best and most read novels by Amazon and the Washington Post.
