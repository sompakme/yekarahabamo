Highlights

  • Amy Adams is a versatile actress with success in a variety of genres, including comedy, musicals, and dramas.
  • The highest-grossing Amy Adams movies include Batman v Superman and Man Of Steel .
  • Her performances in films like Arrival and American Hustle show her growth beyond romantic comedies and her ability to lead ambitious and critically acclaimed films.

Famed for her skill in both dramatic and comedic roles, Amy Adams is widely regarded as one of the most gifted and multi-genre actresses working today.

She may not have won an Oscar yet, but she has received six nominations. Adams has starred in some critically acclaimed films, and this article ranks her top ten highest-grossing films of all time.

The variety of Adams' filmography shows that she is popular in many different types of films, including superhero action flicks, musicals, and family comedies.

Adams has brought warmth and relatability to the screen in her roles as numerous iconic characters, including a courageous journalist, a Disney princess, and many more.

10 Talladega Nights (2016)

Total Box Office Gross: $162.8 Million

The 2006 NASCAR comedy Talladega Nights: The Ballad Of Ricky Bobby features the comedic talents of Will Ferrell as the unforgettable titular race car driver, accompanied by Amy Adams in the role of his dedicated assistant, Susan.

The movie soared to Ferrell's most successful live-action film upon its debut, featuring a delightful blend of quick-witted humor and a stellar ensemble of comedic talents.

Talladega Nights proved to be another box office success in Ferrell and Adam McKay's comedic collaboration, grossing an impressive $162.8 million worldwide on a budget of over $72 million.

9 The Muppets (2011)

Total Box Office Gross: $160.9 Million

The 2011 musical comedy The Muppets, featuring Amy Adams and Jason Segel, received a favorable 75 Metascore and achieved a remarkable worldwide box office gross of $160.9 million, all while working on a modest budget of $45 million.

Mary, played by Adams, captivated the audience with her delightful singing and dancing in this charming small-town setting.

A duet she performed with Kermit for the subtly ironic ditty "Life's A Happy Song" perfectly captured the film's joyful and self-aware atmosphere, which impressed both critics and audiences.

This showcased Adams' ability to excel in different genres.

8 Arrival (2016)

Total Box Office Gross: $202 Million

Arrival, featuring Amy Adams as an expert in decoding an extraterrestrial language, grossed $202 million worldwide on a $47 million budget.

The picture combined spectacle with emotion, earning an 81 Metascore and eight Oscar nominations, including one in the category of "Best Picture."

Adams grounded Arrival's investigation of communication and togetherness with humanity and vulnerability as linguistics specialist Dr. Louise Banks. Adams' emotional versatility in a complicated lead role showed her growth beyond romantic comedies.

Arrival demonstrated that Adams could lead more ambitious, effects-driven films with exquisite imagery.

7 American Hustle (2013)

Total Box Office Gross: $257 Million

American Hustle, featuring Amy Adams and Bradley Cooper, was a commercial success in 2013, grossing over $257 million worldwide and collecting ten Academy Award nominations.

Adams exhibited tremendous acting and physical range as the masterfully manipulative con woman Sydney Prosser, featuring show-stopping plunging necklines and a thick Bronx accent; the film continued her hot record of award-season success in the early 2010s.

American Hustle confirmed Adams as a leading lady who could deftly balance drama and comedy, riding on fantastic reviews and star power.

6 Enchanted (2007)

Total Box Office Gross: $340.3 Million

Amy Adams' breakout role as the computerized fairytale princess Giselle in 2007's live-action-musical Enchanted, starring Patrick Dempsey, grossed over $340 million worldwide.

The fish-out-of-water comedy won Adams her first of three Golden Globe Awards as the guileless, show-stopping Giselle, blending her magnificent singing voice with goofy physical comedy.

Enchanted's 75 Metascore and two Oscar nominations propelled Adams to stardom, demonstrating her unusual range with crack comedic intonation, and vocals strong enough to front a Disney blockbuster as lovable as its fairytale protagonist.

5 Catch Me If You Can (2002)

Total Box Office Gross: $355.6 Million

The 2002 historical crime film Catch Me If You Can, directed by Steven Spielberg and featuring Leonardo DiCaprio and Tom Hanks as the lead, was a tremendous hit, earning a staggering $355.6 million worldwide.

Amy Adams delivers a standout performance as Brenda, the youthful nurse who becomes enamored with Frank Abagnale Jr., portrayed by DiCaprio.

Her positive outlook and cheerful demeanor complemented Frank's daring and impulsive nature. Adams skillfully added a touch of empathy to the couple's romance, which served as a grounding element amid the stylish, retro chase sequences.

Catch Me If You Can beautifully captured the essence of the 1960s, showcasing Adams' incredible versatility and solidifying its place as Spielberg's most successful drama on a global scale.

4 Night At The Museum: Battle Of The Smithsonian (2009)

Total Box Office Gross: $402.2 Million

With over $402 million worldwide revenue, the 2009 adventurous comedy sequel Night At The Museum: Battle Of The Smithsonian outperformed its predecessor.

Amy Adams excelled in the role of Amelia Earhart in this film, which featured Ben Stiller, Owen Wilson, and Meryl Streep, among others.

While not appearing in a leading role, Adams enlivened the crew with her courageous charisma and hilarious physical comedy while guiding them.

By capitalizing on its star power and family-friendly nature, Night At The Museum: Battle Of The Smithsonian solidified Adams' prowess in directing blockbuster tentpoles that rely heavily on precise comic ensemble work.

3 Justice League (2017)

Total Box Office Gross: $655.9 Million

Despite mixed reviews, the 2017 DC superhero team-up film Justice League, starring Ben Affleck and Gal Gadot, managed to bag over $655.9 million globally.

Reprising her role as intrepid journalist Lois Lane, Amy Adams anchored the movie's emotional arc with a grounded performance navigating Lois's grief.

While highlights went to spectacles like Ezra Miller's Flash, Adams brought her signature heart and pluckiness amid franchise-building bombast.

Though not as lucrative as entries in other "cinematic universes," Justice League demonstrated Adams' enduring appeal even as Lois took a backseat to Superman's death and resurrection.

2 Man Of Steel (2013)

Total Box Office Gross: $667.9 Million

In 2013, the franchise rebooting origin story Man Of Steel introduced Henry Cavill as Superman, alongside Amy Adams as the intrepid reporter Lois Lane.

While overshadowed by the CGI fight sequences, Adams delivered a heartfelt performance as the woman who unwaveringly believes in Superman's inherent goodness.

Despite mixed reviews, Man Of Steel proved to be a box office success, surpassing the earnings of all previous Superman movies.

Adams' portrayal of Lois showcases her incredible range and relatability, even in big-budget films prioritizing action over character development.

1 Batman v Superman: The Dawn Of Justice (2016)

Total Box Office Gross: $873.6 Million

In 2016, the highly anticipated clash between DC superheroes Batman and Superman in Batman v Superman: The Dawn Of Justice proved to be a great success at the box office, earning a staggering $873 million globally.

Amy Adams portrayed the character of Lois Lane with a strong and principled demeanor, bringing a sense of morality to the intense and dramatic clash.

Despite playing a supporting role, Adams delivered a performance that touched viewers' hearts with differing opinions on a gargantuan budget of $250,000,000.

Her exceptional performance heightened the emotional intensity of the battle, showcasing her undeniable ability to captivate the audience, and Batman v Superman: The Dawn Of Justice is Amy Adams' highest-grossing movie to date.
