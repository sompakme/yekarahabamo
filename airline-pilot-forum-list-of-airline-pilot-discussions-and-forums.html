    Etiquette and Guidelines for Using the Airline Pilot Forum

    The airline pilot forum maintains a set of guidelines and rules to foster a respectful and productive environment for all users. By adhering to these guidelines, participants can ensure that discussions on the forum remain constructive, informative, and conducive to the growth of the aviation community.

    1. Respectful and Positive Tone

    When engaging in discussions on the airline pilot forum, it is crucial to maintain a respectful tone towards fellow participants. Keep in mind that everyone’s experiences and perspectives may differ, so it is essential to engage in a courteous and open-minded manner. Avoid derogatory or offensive language and refrain from personal attacks.

    2. Unauthorized Labor Actions

    The forum strictly prohibits any advocacy or discussions related to unauthorized labor actions. This includes urging or planning strikes, sick-outs, or any other form of collective job action. Violating this rule can have serious consequences, including legal actions by major unions and potential repercussions on your professional career.

    3. Use of Company Email Addresses

    For accuracy and verification purposes, the airline pilot forum requires the use of company email addresses for submissions. This ensures the authenticity of the information shared on the forum and helps maintain a reliable and trustworthy platform for all participants.

    4. Constructive Contributions

    Contribute to the forum by sharing valuable insights, experiences, and information relevant to the aviation community. Engage in meaningful discussions and provide helpful solutions or suggestions when addressing fellow participants’ queries or concerns. By fostering a collaborative environment, we can collectively enhance our knowledge and understanding of the aviation industry.
